# Behavioral Profile

## Overview
Jason's profile is that of a **Strategic Builder** — someone who operates equally well as a hands-on technical architect and as a strategic leader who shapes direction. He is energised by founding-stage challenges, high-stakes infrastructure, and teams where AI-augmented development is a first-class concern, not an afterthought.

## Core Behavioral Drives

| Drive | Level | Meaning |
|-------|-------|---------|
| Autonomy | High | Needs ownership; performs best with clear scope and minimal micromanagement |
| Technical Depth | High | Driven by hard problems — latency, scale, distributed systems, protocol design |
| Innovation | High | Energised by 0-to-1 builds; finds pure maintenance work draining |
| Strategic Influence | High | Wants to shape technical direction, not just execute on it |

## Strongest Behaviors
- **Systems Thinking:** Naturally zooms out to architecture and long-term design while maintaining delivery velocity
- **Cross-Functional Communication:** Bridges engineering and C-level; translates technical trade-offs into business terms
- **AI-Augmented Development:** Treats Claude Code and agentic workflows as a core architectural pillar — builds systems designed for AI-assisted iteration
- **Speed with Rigour:** Operates at startup velocity without sacrificing institutional-grade engineering standards

## How You Work Best
- Fully remote, async-first teams with strong ownership culture
- Greenfield or early-stage products where architectural decisions still matter
- Founding or staff-level scope: close to the product, close to the business
- High-performance systems domains: trading, DeFi, fintech, infrastructure
- Teams that treat AI tooling as a competitive advantage

## Growth Areas (frame positively in applications)
- **Scale of team management:** Background is primarily technical leadership and architectural authority rather than large headcount management — frame as a strength in lean, high-impact team structures
- **Formal PM processes:** Comes from founder/builder culture; highly outcome-driven rather than process-driven — frame as delivery focus and pragmatism

## Mapping to Job Posting Language

When a job posting mentions these keywords, it's a **strong behavioral fit**:
- "founding engineer", "staff engineer", "principal engineer"
- "greenfield", "0-to-1", "early stage", "ground up"
- "high ownership", "autonomy", "self-directed"
- "high-performance", "low latency", "real-time systems"
- "AI-augmented", "agentic", "AI tooling"
- "remote-first"

When a job posting mentions these, flag as **potential friction** (not deal-breaker):
- "on-site", "hybrid", "in-office" — deal-breaker, not just friction
- "large matrixed organisation" — may lack the ownership Jason thrives on
- "primarily maintenance and support" — low energy alignment
- "strong process adherence" — worth clarifying whether this means bureaucratic overhead

## Management Style Preferences
- Prefers leaders who set clear outcomes and get out of the way
- Works well with technical founders and CTOs who are close to the product
- Does not thrive under micromanagement or highly political environments

## Using This in Applications
- **Cover letters:** Lead with the builder/architect identity; highlight ownership and agentic development philosophy
- **CV:** Emphasise greenfield builds, scale numbers, and the breadth from smart contracts to infrastructure to React
- **Interviews:** Use STAR examples that show both technical depth and strategic influence
- **Don't overstate:** Avoid implying large direct report headcount — the strength is technical authority and architectural ownership, not headcount management
